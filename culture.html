Tradition, memory, and ceremony

Balla Lompoa is not only a place for stored objects. It is also linked to living cultural practices and the memory of the Gowa Kingdom.

Living heritage

The cultural value of the museum grows from the relationship between heirlooms, ceremonies, local identity, and public education.

  • Accera KalompoangA traditional ceremony connected with the cleansing and care of royal regalia.
  • Makassar identityThe museum helps explain language, leadership, architecture, and court customs.
  • EducationStudents and visitors can connect local history with visible objects.
